What are the Ways to Increase Footfall in Your Trade Show Booth?

With so many booths vying for the attention of the attendees, it’s difficult to stand out. As an exhibitor, you need to be smart in your approach to make the best use of your booth space. The secret of captivating your visitor’s attention is to design your exhibition stand in the best possible manner. Make your booth social-friendly and conduct exciting user-engagement activities to keep visitor interest intact. The result would be higher footfall in your booth.

You can hire professionals to get the best booth rental for your brand and exhibition. Once you get a captivating booth, you should follow the following strategies and suggestions to boost the footfall in your trade show booth.

● Set Goals for the Event

Every task should be done with a vision. Your trade show strategies need to be aligned with your event goals. This approach helps to create the look and feel of your 20x20 trade show booth, enhance engagement, and create lead generation strategies and follow-up plans. As an exhibitor, you may have various reasons to participate in an event. It may be increasing brand awareness or generating leads. Some brands opt to improve their relationship with their current clients. Some exhibitors use an event to educate their existing and potential customers about their brands and products.

Setting a distinct exhibition goal makes it easier to organize, market, and measure the function. Whatever goal you set, it must be specific, measurable, achievable, and result-oriented. You also need to determine which key performance indicators (KPIs) are the best to measure the results. It will help you determine what worked in your favor, and what should be modified for future events. In some events, gross revenue is the best measure, and for some events, you may use guest registrations to measure the result. If you establish the goals early in the process, you can easily prioritize the tasks and make your event a huge success.

● Invite Special Guests

Events invite special guests, such as industry veterans, dignitaries, or keynote speakers to increase footfall in your booth. Bring your guest into the planning stage, well ahead of the show. This step ensures that each dignitary has sufficient information needed to make your event a huge success.

Whatever initiatives you want to take ensure you take it well ahead of the show. If your event is built around a special guest or speaker, ensure you arrive early to make them available on the desired date. You can include information about this dignitary in early promotional materials to generate curiosity and interest among your potential clients. The same strategy can be applied to the sponsors as well. Once a sponsor or guest has been signed on, you can share your event and reach a broader audience.

Such sessions encourage other professionals in the crowd to discuss your product and services during their conversation. This phenomenon strengthens your brand image among your booth visitors.
