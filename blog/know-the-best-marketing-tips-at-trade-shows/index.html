3 Powerful Marketing Tips to Get More Out of Your Trade Shows

Trade shows offer incredible opportunities for your business, such as spreading brand awareness, direct marketing, lead generation, and many more. The most advantageous one among all these is direct marketing. Trade fairs and expos are a great platform for direct marketing through face-to-face interactions, allowing you to generate potential leads and boost sales.

It might sound easy, but result-oriented direct marketing requires proper communication skills and a comprehensive strategy. Most exhibitors are clueless about effective marketing through visitor engagement and can’t seize the opportunity for their business. To help you in such a situation, we are here to share some valuable insights with you.

We are mentioning a few useful trade show marketing tips here. Using them during visitor interactions will increase the likelihood of turning your potential customers into loyal ones.

Engage Visitors in Simple and Result-Oriented Conversations

The easy way to interact with visitors is to hand out testers and give demonstrations on how your products work and how they can be beneficial. Along with this, you also need to filter those people who will buy that product from you. You cannot make this happen by asking them a million questions or making them fill in complicated forms.

The best way to qualify your leads is to simplify your conversations, ask about their expectations of the product, and tell them how your product can fulfill their needs. You just need to ensure that these questions flow naturally within the conversation so that you don’t seem like pushing them to buy your product.

Don’t Just Exchange Business Cards

Handing out business cards is a traditional method for networking and sales, but in a way, they add friction to your exhibit booth interactions. With business cards, you place the burden on the visitor to get in contact, creating friction. Remember that if you have to convert a potential customer, you should never hand them a business card and send them on their way, hoping they will call you.

Employ different systems to remove the friction. One such way is to use something like a QR code, a website URL, a mobile app, or some other digital tool. Meanwhile, you can take a picture of their business card and put details instantly into your CRM. This will encourage your visitors to connect with you freely, and you can follow up with them later.

Get Dedicated Booth Space for On-site Meetings

One assured way to enhance interaction is to have a meeting room in your exhibition booth. However, you can schedule meetings after the event, but having an on-site meeting room gives you the flexibility to hold meetings with potential clients right then and there. It also eliminates the need to plan meetings for later and to work around each other’s diaries. Instead, you can head straight into the onsite meeting room and close the deal.

If your budget doesn’t allow you to build separate onsite meeting rooms, put appropriate furniture pieces in place to invite your visitors to sit and discuss without hesitation. This step will make it easier to interact with your potential clients, generate sales, and close deals.
